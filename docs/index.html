Yeamin Faria Chowdhury is a geospatial scientist specializing in wildlife ecology, remote sensing, and spatial data science. She earned a PhD in Geospatial Information Sciences from The University of Texas at Dallas, USA. Her dissertation used camera-trap, environmental, and anthropogenic data to study mammal behavior and predator–prey dynamics in Guyana. She develops machine learning workflows in Python, R, and ArcGIS. She has teaching experience in GIS and analytics, and has received the 2024–25 Pioneer Student Research Grant and the 2025 GIS Outstanding Teaching Assistant Award.

Education:
PhD in Geospatial Information Sciences (August 2025). The University of Texas at Dallas, Texas, USA.

Master of Science in Environmental Sciences (December 2019) Jahangirnagar University, Savar, Bangladesh.
